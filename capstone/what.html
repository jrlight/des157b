What is Hormone Replacement Therapy?

First, we need to understand how the body works. Here are some very important words:

    Hormones are chemicals that tell your body how to do certain functions. Think of them as chemical messengers!

Sex hormones facilitate sexual differentiation and reproduction. They drive the formation and growth of primary sex characteristics (genitals) and secondary sex characteristics (chest size, hair growth, fat distribution).

Androgens (Testosterone)

  • The most common androgen is the hormone testosterone. In medium to large amounts, testosterone leads to masculinizing. However, testosterone is present in all sexes— small amounts of testosterone are important for keeping anyone healthy. Testes generally make large amounts of testosterone, and ovaries generally to make small testosterone.

Estrogen

  • In medium to large amounts, these hormones lead to masculinizing. However, everyone makes some androgens (even non-intersex cis women).

Progesterone

  • In medium to large amounts, these hormones lead to masculinizing. However, everyone makes some androgens (even non-intersex cis women).

Gonads are the organs that produce sex hormones. There are two types of gonads in humans— ovaries and testes. Additionally, some people have Gonadal Dysgenesis, where gonads never develop into either, and instead become undifferentiated "streak" gonads that do not produce hormones.
